# Morning Supplement Stack (Example)

An example supplement stack to show how LifeOS tracks supplements. Customize this with your own stack.

## Added Supplements

### Caffeine + L-Theanine

Label read:

- Natural caffeine: `100 mg`
- L-theanine: `200 mg`

Likely purpose:

- short-term alertness
- focus
- reduced drowsiness
- calmer stimulation than caffeine alone for some people

Important notes:

- Caffeine can worsen anxiety, sleep, palpitations, reflux, and blood pressure in sensitive people.
- Better as a morning supplement than a late-day supplement.

### Actazin Kiwifruit Extract

Label read:

- H-Actazin kiwifruit powder: `100 mg`

Likely purpose:

- digestive support
- bowel regularity
- gut comfort

Important notes:

- This is more of a digestion/gut supplement than an energy supplement.
- Avoid if kiwifruit triggers allergy or oral itching.

### Alpha GPC

Label read:

- Alpha GPC: `300 mg`

Likely purpose:

- choline support
- acetylcholine support
- possible cognition/focus support
- possible neuromuscular/performance support

Important notes:

- Evidence is still developing.
- Choline-related supplements can sometimes cause headache, GI upset, sweating, fishy body odor, or low blood pressure in some people.

### Organic India Brahmi / Gotu Kola

Label read:

- Brahmi/Gotu Kola style herbal supplement
- Label visible: `Centella asiatica 350 mg` per capsule

Likely purpose:

- traditional cognitive support
- calm/stress support
- memory/attention support over time

Important notes:

- "Brahmi" can refer to different herbs depending on product: `Bacopa monnieri`, `Centella asiatica`, or blends.
- This label visibly mentions `Centella asiatica`.
- Herbal products can vary a lot by brand and batch.
- Stop and check with a clinician if liver symptoms appear, such as dark urine, yellowing eyes/skin, unusual fatigue, or right-upper-abdominal pain.

### Wellbeing Nutrition Slow Multi + Omega for Him

Label read:

- multivitamin/mineral blend
- omega
- probiotics/prebiotics
- ginseng
- milk thistle
- lycopene
- beta carotene
- CoQ10
- lutein/zeaxanthin

Likely purpose:

- broad micronutrient coverage
- omega support
- gut microbiome support
- general wellness support

Important notes:

- Multivitamins are best treated as gap-fillers, not replacements for food.
- Probiotic effects are strain-specific.
- Omega benefits depend on actual EPA/DHA content and dose.

## Sources Used

- MedlinePlus: caffeine is a central nervous system stimulant and may be used short term for fatigue or drowsiness.
- NIH Office of Dietary Supplements: choline is involved in acetylcholine synthesis, cell membrane structure, and nervous system function.
- PubMed: Alpha-GPC is a choline donor; cognition/performance research is ongoing.
- PubMed/PMC: kiwifruit interventions have been studied for gut health and bowel function.
- NCCIH: probiotics may be useful for some digestive conditions, but effects depend on the organism and product.
- NIH Office of Dietary Supplements: omega-3 fatty acids are important parts of cell membranes and include EPA/DHA.

## Practical Morning Stack Interpretation

This stack looks like:

- one stimulation/focus item: caffeine + L-theanine
- one choline/focus item: Alpha GPC
- one digestive item: Actazin kiwifruit
- one herbal calm/cognition item: Brahmi/Gotu Kola
- one broad coverage item: Slow Multi + Omega
